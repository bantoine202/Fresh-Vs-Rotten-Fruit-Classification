Fresh vs Rotten Fruit Classification

Using Deep Learning to Identify Fresh and Rotten Produce

Problem Method Experiments Results Takeaways

Problem Description & Motivation

Food waste is a major economic and environmental issue. Consumers, grocery stores, and restaurants often visually inspect food quality, which is subjective, inconsistent, and time-consuming. Automatically detecting rotten produce could support:

  • Reducing waste and cost
  • Improving food safety
  • Automating sorting in supply chains

This project applies **deep learning image classification** to detect whether fruit is fresh or rotten using consumer-grade images.

The goal is to design a **fast, lightweight, accurate model with real-world potential**.

Technical Approach

Dataset

The dataset consists of images of fresh and rotten apples, bananas, and oranges. These were organized into a binary classification problem: fresh vs rotten.

Model Architecture: AlexNet

The project uses AlexNet, a classic convolutional neural network, fine-tuned with transfer learning. The final fully connected layer was replaced with a 2-class output layer.

Preprocessing & Augmentation

Images were resized to 224×224 and normalized. Augmentations included:

  • Random flips
  • Color jitter
  • Random rotation

Training Setup

  • Loss: CrossEntropy
  • Optimizer: Adam
  • Epochs: 5
  • Tracked metrics: accuracy, loss

Tracking & Visualization

All experiments were logged using Weights & Biases, including accuracy, loss, confusion matrices, and run comparisons.

Experiments

Four experiments were conducted on AlexNet and ResNet18:

  1. Batch size: 16 vs 64
  2. Learning rate: 1e-3 vs 1e-4
  3. Augmentation: on vs off
  4. Architecture: AlexNet vs ResNet18

This allowed measurement of model robustness, training dynamics, and generalization.

Conclusions & Takeaways

1. Batch Size

Smaller batches produced slightly smoother convergence, but both sizes converged.

2. Learning Rate

Lower learning rates improved stability and prevented noisy updates.

3. Data Augmentation

Augmentation improved generalization and reduced overfitting risk.

4. AlexNet vs ResNet18

ResNet18 trained faster and reached strong performance, but AlexNet was sufficiently accurate and computationally lighter.

Overall Finding

Deep learning can reliably detect fruit quality with extremely high accuracy using lightweight architectures and modest datasets.
